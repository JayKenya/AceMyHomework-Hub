Best format for College Essay: Writing guide, Outline, and Template

Acemyhomework Blog Feb 23, 2022

Choosing the appropriate college essay format to use with your assignment can be a challenge. The structure selected matters a lot since it determines whether your academic paper will be approved. Sadly, many students still use the wrong format and lose valuable points.

How do you format a good college essay? Here is a brief overview of the most popular essay formats used in college and what they look like.

What is the Format of a College Essay?

Most people want to know, “are college essays in MLA format?” Well, not necessarily. Sometimes the student may be required to write their essay in APA, Chicago or Harvad formats. This all depends on the instructions given. 

The following is an example format for college application essay:

  • Title: "Why I want to be [insert school here]." This can be changed to whatever makes sense for your essay.
  • Paragraph 1: Introduction: Introduce yourself and explain why you want to attend this school (pick something specific.)
  • Paragraph 2: Storytelling: Talk about something that has happened in your life that has shaped who you are.
  • Paragraph 3: Storytelling: Talk about something that has happened in your life that has shaped who you are.
  • Paragraph 4: Storytelling: Talk about something that has happened in your life that has shaped who you are.
  • Paragraph 5: Storytelling: Talk about something that has happened in your life that has shaped who you are.

Conclusion: Wrap up everything in the paragraphs above into one sentence. 

How to Write an Essay for College Admission

Instead of starting with the introduction and trying to figure out what your essay will cover, start with your argument. Make a list of all the reasons you want to go to grad school or all the reasons why you think you shouldn't go.

Your reasons are the major themes in your essay, so choose the most important and interesting ones for you. The next step is to structure your argument. If you have four main ideas you want to present, you will need four main paragraphs in the body of your essay.

Once you know what your body paragraphs will be about, it's easy to write an introduction and conclusion. Your introduction should give an idea of why you're writing the essay.

Is it because you want to get into grad school, or someone told you that you needed a higher degree? Afterwards, give a preview of what's coming up in each paragraph. This lets the reader know what they're going to learn from reading your paper.

Introductory Paragraph

If you have just been assigned a paper and don't know where to start, gather some samples of introductory paragraphs and a college essay header. This will help you learn the essential components of a preceding paragraph and identify them in other people's writing.

Once you've got the basics down, try writing your introductory paragraph without looking at any examples. The introductory paragraph comprises three parts:

  • Hook
  • Bridge
  • Thesis statement

The hook grabs the reader's attention and pulls them into your essay. The bridge connects the hook to the thesis statement, which introduces the main idea of your essay. The bridge usually consists of one or two sentences that elaborate on your hook.

The thesis statement contains one sentence that summarizes your entire paper. It tells readers what the essay is about and what they can expect as they read further. Use a few different hooks until you find one that sounds best with your thesis statement.

Supporting Paragraphs

The supporting paragraphs of a college essay should do precisely what their name suggests: support the central idea. When carefully constructed, these paragraphs provide evidence to back up your thesis and keep your argument on the topic.

In the first sentence of each paragraph, you should give readers an idea of the subject you will discuss and how it relates to your thesis. The following sentences should offer detailed evidence, facts, statistics, and examples to support your point.

Finally, close each paragraph with a sentence that transitions into the next one. After all, your argument would be meaningless if readers don't understand how it fits together.

  • Each piece of evidence in a solid essay supports the thesis statement. If they don't support it, they probably shouldn't be there at all. Provide plenty of evidence:
  • Without evidence to back up your arguments, readers won't take them seriously. Solid essays provide evidence from academic journals and books written by experts from credible sources.

Conclusion Paragraphs

If you're wondering how to write a conclusion for a college essay, you've come to the right place. Writing the conclusion of your essay is one of the essential parts of writing a successful college essay. It should be short but powerful and memorable.

The conclusion of your essay is where you will leave your reader with a lasting impression. The first step in writing a reasonable conclusion is to read over the entire essay and make sure that it flows well and that each paragraph has a purpose.

Then move on to the conclusion. The best way to start your conclusion is to restate your thesis statement. This reminds the audience what you were trying to prove in your essay.

Then, use an anecdote or personal experience related to your thesis statement. Lastly, end with a strong sentence that will stick with your reader after reading your essay.

Personal Essay for College Format

There are many theories about the "best format for college essay"- the key is to find what works for you. Start by understanding your goals and getting a feel for each of these approaches and their pros and cons.

Once you consider what you need for your situation, feel free to write in the style that works best for you. Additionally, you can use all these styles together in one essay.
