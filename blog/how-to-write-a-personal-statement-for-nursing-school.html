How to Write a Personal Statement for Nursing School

Acemyhomework Blog Feb 23, 2022

Everyone looks forward to having a great career, but a phenomenal career is mainly associated with a great deal of academic work. Nursing is no exemption to this. When applying to a nursing school, providing a personal nursing statement can help your admission process. 

A personal statement gives you a chance to elaborate on experiences and interests that are never included in your application. Creating a unique personal statement is critical in your nursing school journey. Read on as we assist you in defining what a personal nursing statement is and how to craft one of your own

Personal Statements for Nursing School

Before we can get to the writing bit, how about we learn what a personal statement is?

A personal statement is an admission or application essay written by a prospective student applying to a college or university. It is a critical essay as part of the application process. This aside, you will require your transcripts and letters of recommendation.

Now that you know what a personal statement is, here are the steps you need in writing a nursing school personal statement:

  • Settle on your schools.

 It all starts with choosing the perfect programs you would like to apply to, researching the best nursing school that aligns with your skills, careers, interests, and values. Seek advice from a counselor of each school.

  • Create a timeframe

Make a doable timeline for yourself with achievable goals for completing numerous components of your personal statement and the other application sectors. Put down all the deadlines that the engaging schools have to stay organized to manage time effectively.

  • Plan a personal statement

Have creative ideas for the nursing school personal statement before you embark on the essay. Perform some creative writing exercises that might assist you in making the written statement. Just write without stopping, have a beginning, middle, and end part of your essay.

  • Focus on your approach

Think about how you want the admitting committee to experience your essay. Start with an experience captivating and mesmerizing enough to capture their attention. Remember, the transition matter from one place to another.

  • Use precise examples and details

Include specific details, narratives, or examples in your statement. Specific details are easy to memorize than generalized ones. Describe your attribute without mentioning the exact attribute; select a scenario that depicts just that.

  • Get feedback

Remember, the goal is to have one of your selected schools pick you based on the essay you have written, so have the essay thoroughly checked. Ask as many people as possible to criticize and give you the first impression of your work.

  • Revise and Proofread.

Significant work has been proofread before submission, edit the whole work and revise any content on your essay, including your transitions. Finally, check the spelling, grammar, and punctuation. Remember, you need a perfect personal statement nursing school has ever seen.

How Long Is a Personal Statement for Nursing?

 Remember, you do not need to write everything about yourself; your statement should be clear and have a word limit of about 500-1000 words; taking only one A4 paper, keep it precise, clear, and easy to read.

Like all the essays, you need an introduction that gives a brief history of why you chose nursing. The middle part should include the experiences, educational background, and skills. If given a chance, the conclusion should have what you intend to achieve and what you are bringing to the table.

Nursing School Personal Statement Examples

Coming up with the best examples of a personal statement, you will need to know which areas your focal points should be based on. How can I make myself stand out for nursing school? This question is one of the most frequently asked by every applicant looking for a chance to impress the admission committee. 

So here are a few points that we have outlined for you to ensure you leave just the mark that you intend to:

  • Education

Discuss your previous education experience and the relevance it has to nursing. Mention some courses you did, mention any relevant certificates you have connected to nursing, and list the practical skills you acquired and how they can act as a pivoting ground for your inspiration.

  • Volunteer

Include the volunteer experiences you had, especially those related to nursing, listing your responsibilities and what lessons you learned throughout the process.

  • Work experience

Put down the various working experiences you had, even if it was not related to nursing, harness the skills you achieved that could play a massive role in nursing, like teamwork.

  • Relevant skills

In this place, discuss more soft skills that make you feel alive and ready to face the day and could lift your career in the nursing industry.

  • Personal motivation

Consider putting down your motivation in these areas lean more on the emotional connection you have to nursing. Maybe it was your dad's career, and he inspired you.

  • Unique traits

What do you consider to make you shine in the world? What makes you stand out? Well, ensure you make that trait known, as that will be your identity.

The above points will make your essay stand out among others while giving you an edge to even the field.

Nursing School Essay Sample

Having understood what you require, here is a short guideline of what a nursing sample essay can look like.

"As this was my first day working at the garage, I was all psyched up and ready for action as it was my granddad's dream, little did I know it was going to pave my career in a light of an entirely different dimension…."

Show what transpired that made you change your mind, indulge the reader in what you went through during the change.
